How to Change the Tyre of a Car

A simple, step-by-step guide for safe tyre replacement

Tools You Will Need

  • Spare tyre
  • Car jack
  • Wheel wrench or lug wrench
  • Wheel chocks (to prevent rolling)
  • Gloves and safety vest (optional but recommended)

Step-by-Step Guide to Changing a Car Tyre

1. Park Safely and Turn on Hazard Lights

Move the car to a flat surface away from traffic. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers.

2. Secure the Vehicle

Apply the handbrake and place wheel chocks behind the tyres to prevent the car from moving.

3. Loosen the Lug Nuts

Use the wheel wrench to slightly loosen the lug nuts before lifting the car. Do not remove them completely yet.

4. Lift the Car Using the Jack

Place the jack under the car’s designated jacking point and raise the car until the flat tyre is off the ground.

5. Remove the Flat Tyre and Install the Spare

Completely remove the lug nuts, take off the flat tyre, and replace it with the spare. Hand-tighten the lug nuts first.

6. Lower the Car and Fully Tighten the Lug Nuts

Lower the car slowly and then use the wheel wrench to securely tighten all the lug nuts in a star pattern.

Safety Tips

  • Always carry a spare tyre and proper tools in your car.
  • Do not change a tyre on a slope or soft ground.
  • If you are on a busy road, consider calling roadside assistance for safety.

Conclusion

Changing a car tyre is a useful skill that can save you time and stress. With the right tools and safety measures, you can replace a flat tyre and get back on the road confidently.
